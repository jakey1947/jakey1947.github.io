Tikki Takka

Mystic Beats

Fight against your enemies through the power of Rhythm.
Each spell is a different beat, learn them, master them, and destroy your enemies through a mix of dexterity and strategy never seen before.
Tikki Takka is a Rhythm Fighting game where each spell is a pattern which the player must beat out on their drums to cast, there are currently only a small selection of spells available but the intent in the future is for there to be enough variety for players to create different play styles and strategies with their own strengths and weaknesses.

Rhythm Detection

Each spell in Tikki Takka is cast by playing their chart on your drum, similar to how a traditional rhythm game work. However, you can cast any spell at any time so we needed a system to make sure the spell the player is trying to cast, is cast when they want to.

Step one is input, how do we know which pattern the player is playing? I did this by storing a list of the deltas between each input. The max length being the length of the longest spell in the players deck. each time the player hits their drum, I iterate through the list of spells and match the inputs against those deltas. I do this for every delta that fits within the spell chart to determine the progress through the spell.

While the progress is being stored, if a spell is complete it is stored for later, and only overridden by spells which are longer than the current completed spell. If a spell has more progress than the current completed spell, it won't be cast immediately but instead will be stored until the next input where it'll be cast if no spells surpass its length in progress.

AI

The video above is 2 AIs battling each other.

AI in Tikki Takka uses various weights from game state to decide which spells to prioritise, while selecting a spell, a small amount of noise is added to the results to give variance to the selections. The amount of noise is used as a way to control the difficulty of the AI, more noise is less difficult as it doesn't respond to the enemies moves as much compared to no noise which is purely reactive.

The weights are currently set manually in the editor but I may create a trainer at some point.
